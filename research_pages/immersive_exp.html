Immersive Boundary Conditions, Cloaking, Holography, and Cloning

Hereby I summarize a series of laboratory and numerical advances that make it possible to (i) immerse a physical wave experiment into a virtual numerical world (Immersive Boundary Conditions), (ii) remove boundary artefacts in elastic/acoustic experiments (Internal Absorbing Boundaries; MDD), (iii) realise broadband cloaking and holography with real-time control, and (iv) clone arbitrary scatterers so their full scattering response can be recreated on demand.

Elastic Immersive Wave Experimentation (Immersive Boundary Conditions)

Immersive boundary conditions (IBCs) let waves travel seamlessly between a physical laboratory and a virtual numerical domain. We derived and validated an iterative scheme for elastic media using only free-surface recordings, enabling cancellation of primary outgoing waves and synthesis of physical–virtual interactions without interior access.

  • Contribution: Iterative IBC for 3D elastic problems using only surface data; validated with 2D synthetic experiments.
  • Why it matters: Enables “infinite” lab experiments at seismic-band frequencies by removing reflection bottlenecks.

Key paper: Li, X., Robertsson, J., and van Manen, D.-J., Elastic immersive wave experimentation, Geophys. J. Int., 2023.

Internal Absorbing Boundaries for Closed-Aperture Wavefield Separation

We introduced an internal absorbing boundary condition (IABC) that allows full separation of outgoing elastic wavefield constituents from data recorded on a closed free surface, even when the solid’s interior is unknown. The method injects surface data into an FD model and annihilates ingoing energy internally, preventing destructive interference that otherwise blocks separation of higher-order components.

  • Contribution: First closed-aperture elastic wavefield separation retrieving all outgoing orders with unknown interiors.
  • Why it matters: Supplies the incident/outgoing building blocks required by elastic IBCs and lab data processing.

Key paper: Li, X., Robertsson, J., Curtis, A., and van Manen, D.-J., Internal absorbing boundary conditions for closed-aperture wavefield decomposition in solid media with unknown interiors, JASA, 2022.

Elastic Immersive Wavefield Modelling

We extended immersive boundary conditions to elastic numerical modelling so local changes can be studied without re-simulating the entire domain. This reduces computational cost and integrates naturally with IBC experiments and exact extrapolation-by-injection.

  • Contribution: IBCs for elastic wavefield modelling; practical, repeatable subdomain simulations.

Key paper: Li, X., Koene, E., van Manen, D.-J., Robertsson, J., and Curtis, A., Elastic immersive wavefield modelling, J. Comput. Phys., 2022.

Removing Boundary Artefacts with Multidimensional Deconvolution (MDD)

In reduced-size acoustic labs, rigid boundaries cause heavy reverberation. We used MDD to post-process recordings on a closed surface and retrieve the scattering Green’s functions of interior scatterers as if the boundary were transparent. This delivers the radiation-condition datasets needed for broadband cloning and lab holography.

  • Contribution: Closed-aperture unbounded acoustics via MDD; exact interior scattering free of boundary imprint.
  • Why it matters: Supplies the “free-space” scattering response for holography and for lab–numerical hybrid experiments.

Key paper: Li, X., Becker, T., Ravasi, M., Robertsson, J., and van Manen, D.-J., Closed-aperture unbounded acoustics experimentation using multidimensional deconvolution, JASA, 2021.

Broadband Acoustic Cloaking and Holography (Real-time Lab Implementation)

Using IBCs and MDD-derived datasets, we realised real-time cloaking and holography in a 2D acoustic waveguide. The active boundary cancels scattering from real objects (cloak) and recreates the scattering of virtual objects (hologram) across broad frequency bands without a priori knowledge of the source signature.

  • Contribution: First real-time, broadband active cloaking and holography in an acoustic lab.
